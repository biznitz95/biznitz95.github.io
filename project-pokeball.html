[ 01 ] — the brief

Sculpting Poké Balls that print clean.

Poké Balls look simple, but a clean one is all about the seam lines and color breaks — so I model each one with printing in mind from the very first sketch.

The Espeon ball gets sculpted ears and a curling tail; the Great Ball is all about crisp two-tone bands done mid-print — no second extruder, just color swaps timed to the right layers.

It's my favourite way to wind down between the bigger builds.

Build Process

MODEL → PRINT → FINISH
STEP 01

Model it

Designed the shell, ears, and tail in Fusion 360, laying out the geometry so each section prints as its own color without any post-processing.

STEP 02

Print & iterate

More test prints — not just on the Espeon but going back and applying the same improvements to previous pokeballs. Better geometry, cleaner color breaks, tighter fits. Each round informed the next.

STEP 03

Assemble & display

Snapped the pieces together and lined them up on the shelf. Clean result straight from the printer — and the next one's already in the queue.
