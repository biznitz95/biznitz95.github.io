[ 01 ] — the brief

Learning to solder and read schematics.

The goal was simple: pick up a kit, read the schematic, and actually solder it together without burning anything. The coil gun kit from Amazon was the perfect excuse — real components, real traces, real consequences if you bridge the wrong pads.

I got through most of it clean. Then I messed up the capacitor — it ended up sitting too tall and now sticks out the top of the wooden enclosure. The lid won't close.

So now the project has a second chapter: design and print a custom enclosure that fits the actual geometry, adds a window to see the internals, and gives this thing a proper home.

Build Process

SOLDER → OOPS → REDESIGN
STEP 01

Read the schematic

Before picking up the iron, I traced through the full schematic — identifying the charge circuit, the capacitor bank, the fire trigger, and what each component was actually doing. First time reading a real circuit diagram start to finish.

STEP 02

Solder it together

Worked through the through-hole components one by one. Most joints came out clean — but the capacitor ended up soldered at an angle and now sits too tall. The wooden enclosure lid won't shut. Live and learn.

STEP 03

Design a better box

Modeled a custom enclosure in Fusion 360 with full clearance for the tall capacitor, a barrel slot on the front face, and a lid ledge at the top. Currently printing on the X1C.
