[ 01 ] — the brief

A printed supercar that actually drives.

A 911 GT3 RS is mostly curves, so I split the body into bed-sized panels, printed each one, then did the part that really makes or breaks it: bodywork and paint.

Hours of sanding and priming made the printed layer lines vanish, then I laid down candy-purple metallic coats and finished it off with gold wheels — all riding on an RC chassis so it actually drives.

It's the build that taught me patience — the printing is the easy part; the finish is where all the hours go.

Build Process

PRINT → PAINT → DRIVE
STEP 01

Print the body

Started with a complete pre-designed body — no CAD needed. Printed it on the X1C, then bonded and filled the seams into one clean shell.

STEP 02

Bodywork & paint

Sanded and primed until the layer lines disappeared, then laid down candy-purple metallic coats and a clear top coat.

STEP 03

Drivetrain & assembly

Mounted the steering, motor, and battery on the RC platform, then fitted the finished body shell and aero parts on top. Done — and it drives.
